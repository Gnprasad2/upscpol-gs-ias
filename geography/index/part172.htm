Fronts

⧪ When cold air mass and warm air mass blow against each other, the boundary line of convergence separating the two air masses is termed as front. At the boundary, or front, there is a marked drop in temperature, increase in humidity, sudden wind change and a marked pressure rise. This marked discontinuity lends support to the theory of two separate air masses with the front being the boundary between the two.

⧪ When the warm air mass, moves upward over the cold air mass the front formed in such a situation is called warm front. On the contrary, when the cold air mass advances faster and undercuts the warm air mass and forces the warm air upwards, the front so formed is called cold front. The frontal surface of cold front is steeper than that of a warm front.
