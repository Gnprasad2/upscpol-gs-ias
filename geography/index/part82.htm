Seismic Waves or Earthquake Waves

The seismic waves can be classified into two categories:

⧪ Surface Waves: These waves travel through the surface of the earth. Due to their amplitude, they are most destructive waves causing extensive damage on the surface of earth.

♤ Types of Surface Wave

• Love waves (L-waves): It is fastest surface waves and move on ground side to side. It is confined to surface of the crust love wave is wounded by Seismograph.

• Rayleigh waves: Rayleigh waves rolls along the ground just like a wave roll across a lake or an ocean.

• Stoneley waves: Stone waves propagate along a solid - fluid boundary and also along solid

- solid boundary.

• Standing waves: Standing waves are the result of free oscillation of earth. When two waves moving opposite to each other interfere standing waves are produced.

⧪ Body waves: These waves travel through the interiors of the earth. While travelling through interiors, their characteristics such as velocity and wavelength changes according to the density of the medium in which they are travelling. Body Waves can be further categorized into:

♤ Primary Waves: Also known as P-waves. These are longitudinal or compressive in nature. These waves can pass through solid as well as liquid medium. The velocity of these waves increases with increasing density and rigidity of the medium. (They travel faster in solid than in liquids)

♤ Secondary waves: Also known as S-waves. These are transverse or distortional in nature. These waves cannot pass through liquid medium. Their velocity also increases with increasing rigidity of the medium.
