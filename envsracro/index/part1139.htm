3. Elephant Reserves of India


The Indian elephant Elephas maximus occurs in the central and southern Western Ghats, North-east India, eastern India and northern India and in some parts of southern peninsular India. It is distributed in 16 of the 28 states in the country and is showing an increasing trend across its distributional range.

Ocular, upper airway injury, bronchospasm; pulmonary haemorrhage after massive exposure

Petroleum refining

Tracheobronchitis, pulmonary oedema (often delayed onset over 24-48 hours); chronic low level exposure leads to inflammatory changes and emphysema
