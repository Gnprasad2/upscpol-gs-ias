9.1.6. Biodiversity conservation

Do you know?


Not all snakes have fangs. Only the poisonous ones do. Fangs are sharp, long, hollow teeth that are hooked up to small sacs in the snakeâ€™s head behind their eyes. These sacs produce a poisonous liquid called venom. When a snake bites, venom is released and starts to work immediately to kill or paralyze the prey.

Conservation of biological diversity leads to conservation of essential ecological diversity and preserve the continuity of food chains.
