1.3. Good Governance

Governance’ by itself is a neutral term while

`Good Governance’ implies positive attributes and values associated with the quality of governance. Good governance is a dynamic concept and there is much subjectivity involved in defining the aspects of good governance.

United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) recognizes eight core characteristics of good governance:

1. Participatory

2. Consensus oriented

3. Transparent

4. Accountable

5. Responsive

6. Effective and Efficient

7. Equitable and Inclusive

8. Follows the Rule of Law
