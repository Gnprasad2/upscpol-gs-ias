3.2. Components of a Volcano

The volcanoes of explosive type have a volcanic cone, which is formed when the erupted material accumulates around the vent. The vent is an opening of circular or nearly circular shape at the centre of the cone. The vent is connected to the interior of the earth by a narrow pipe. The volcanic materials erupt through this pipe. A funnel-shaped hollow at the top of the cone is called the crater.
